Unit 5: Long-Run Consequences of Stabilization Policies

The Phillips Curve


Things to remember:
  1. Moving across the curve reflects a shift in the AD curve
  2. The SRPC shifts left when SRAS curve shift right, and vice-versa
  3. The LRPC exists at an economy's natural rate of unemployment and resembles that there is no opportunity cost between inflation and unemployment in the long-run

Money Growth and Inflation

Equation for the quantity theory of money:
M x V = P x Y
M=usually M1 money, V=velocity, P=price, Y=real output

Normally V and Y remain constant, so the formula shows that increasing the money supply in the long run only increases nominal GDP/price level, not real-output/RGDP

Deficits and the National Debt

Federal Budget: recorded projection of all gvt. expenditures and revenues over the course of a year, starting in October 1st
Fiscal Stimulus: the use of expansionary fiscal policy
Fiscal Restraint: the use of contractionary fiscal policy
Government Revenue: gvt income from taxes
Government Expenditures: gvt spending and transfer payments
Budget Surplus: when gvt. revenues exceed expenditures
Budget Deficit: when gvt. expenditures exceed revenues
National Debt: the total amount of deficits over several years

Crowding Out

Crowing out is the economic idea that gvt. spending can lead to less spending in the private sector
Gvt. takes loans for expansionary fiscal policy :) -> higher interest rate -> less investment spending -> lower RGDP :(

Economic Growth

Economic Growth happens when the productivity of a nation increases
When the Production Possibilities Curve expands outward or the LRAS curve shifts right, economic growth has ocurred

Factors of Productivity:
  • Technology
  • Amount of Physical Capital (machinery, buildings, computers, etc.)
  • Amount of Human Capital (education, experience, unique skills, etc.)

Public Policy and Economic Growth

There are three types of public policy that cause economic growth:
  1. Increase in education spending: improves human capital
  2. Increase in infrastructure spending: improves infrastructure
  3. Policies that spur innovation: encourages eventual development of new technology
